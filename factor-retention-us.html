The one word to know · baseline
Baseline is the part of the cancel rate the model can't yet attribute to a specific, measured driver — what's left once we strip out everything we can explain. It sits high mostly because we're still missing signals for this group, not because it's a forecast of what "should" happen. Everything above the baseline is the part we can attribute — and act on.
How to read this dashboard
Red contributions push the cancel rate up; green pulls it down. Separately, each cohort carries a model-confidence mark — decision-grade, directional, not yet mature. The bolder the dot, the more we trust the model — it's a confidence signal, not whether the number itself is good or bad.

Within the marketing group, direct sales carries 75% of the effect (+1.72pp) — the single largest add-on to Trial's baseline; organic social (+0.45pp) and SMS (+0.32pp) are minor, and podcast is mildly protective. The signal is which channels bring customers in: direct-sales intake surfaces as cancellations four weeks later (lag 4).
So what
Because the effect acts at a 4-week lag, it's a leading indicator: the at-risk intake is visible weeks before it converts to cancellations. The lever is acquisition quality on the direct-sales channel — who it brings in — not how much is spent.

L3 Transparency · the parked model

The cohort we can't decompose — and why.

We don't hide weak models, we show their limits. Long-Term is nearly half the US book, yet the model has almost nothing to work with here: a single marketing term and a large residual — no product, logistics or payment signal.

Why it's parked
Long-Term's fit is the lowest of any cohort — R² 0.26. The decomposition has just one active group (marketing, +0.1pp, a single search-channel term) plus a residual worth 5.1% of CR — there's no product, logistics or payment structure to attribute. The baseline (4.0%) is actually plausible — just below observed (4.3%) — but with this little structure there's nothing to pull. Verdict: park it — monitor the rate, don't prescribe levers.
