The one word to know · baseline
Baseline is the part of the cancel rate the model can't yet attribute to a measured driver. It includes organic / expected churn, but also factors we can't yet explain. Everything above the baseline is the part we can attribute — and act on.
How to read this dashboard
Red contributions push the cancel rate up; green pulls it down. Separately, each cohort carries a model-confidence mark — decision-grade, directional, not yet mature. The bolder the dot, the more we trust the model — it's a confidence signal, not whether the number itself is good or bad.

Read each row as Observed = Baseline + this week's drivers. Where the baseline is plausible and the fit holds, the decomposition is trustworthy → act. Read
Within the marketing group, offline freebies carries ~80% of the effect (+1.33pp), influencer the remaining ~20% (+0.34pp). The signal is which channels bring customers in — offline-freebies acquisition surfaces as cancellations 2–3 weeks later (lag 2–3).

Why it compounds
The lag-1 term drags 0.31 of last week's entire rate into this week — and last week was itself ~⅔ baseline + its own momentum (carried from the week before). So the term compounds the cohort's standing churn week over week; it isn't remembering a fixable shock. That's why Trial needs an onboarding fix, not a weekly lever.

Baseline 13.7% vs observed 6.7% (249% of CR). To reconcile, recipe_score is pushed to a −175.8% contribution — larger than the cancel rate itself. R² 0.22 masks an unstable structure. Verdict: park it until Sweden accumulates enough Long-Term history.
