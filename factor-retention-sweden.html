Read
Within the marketing group, offline freebies carries ~80% of the effect (+1.33pp), influencer the remaining ~20% (+0.34pp). The signal is which channels bring customers in — offline-freebies acquisition surfaces as cancellations 2–3 weeks later (lag 2–3).
So what
Because the effect acts at a 2–3 week lag, it's a leading indicator: the at-risk intake is visible weeks before it converts to cancellations. The lever is acquisition quality on offline-freebies — who it brings in — not how much is spent.

L3 Transparency · the parked model

Here's the thing we don't trust — and why.

We don't hide weak models, we show their failure mode. Long-Term's baseline overshoots the actual rate by 2.5×, forcing a single product term into a non-physical correction to reconcile.

Failure signature
Baseline 13.7% vs observed 6.7% (249% of CR). To reconcile, recipe_score is pushed to a −175.8% contribution — larger than the cancel rate itself. R² 0.22 masks an unstable structure. Verdict: park it until Sweden accumulates enough Long-Term history.
